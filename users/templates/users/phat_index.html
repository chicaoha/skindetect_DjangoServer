Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequent questions that users often ask

What is skin cancer?

Skin cancer is a disease where malignant (cancerous) cells form in the tissues of the skin. It is the most common type of cancer.

What causes skin cancer?

The primary cause of skin cancer is exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun or tanning beds. Genetic factors and a history of severe sunburns can also contribute.

What are the main types of skin cancer?

The three main types of skin cancer are basal cell carcinoma (BCC), squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), and melanoma. Melanoma is the most aggressive type.

How can I protect my skin from skin cancer?

You can protect your skin by wearing sunscreen, hats and sunglasses, seeking shade, and avoiding tanning beds. Regular skin checks and early detection are also crucial.

What is the prognosis for skin cancer if detected early?

Skin cancer has a high cure rate when detected and treated early. Melanoma, in particular, has a better prognosis when caught in its early stages.

What are the warning signs of skin cancer?

Warning signs include changes in the size, shape, color, or texture of moles or pigmented areas, new growths, or sores that don't heal
